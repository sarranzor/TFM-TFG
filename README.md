## 1- Generation and evaluation of context in emotional images through Foundational Models (2026)

### Generación y evaluación del contexto en imágenes con contenido emocional a través de Modelos Fundacionales (2026)

   Master's Degree in Data Science Thesis | TFM Máster en Ciencida de Datos - Universitat Oberta de Catalunya

  **Abstract**

> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The emergence of foundation models may lead to a paradigm shift in the field of affective computing, with applications in areas such as healthcare and education. The aim of this study is to evaluate the capabilities of foundation models in the recognition of images with context-aware emotions (CAER) and in the synthetic generation of such images.
>
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Firstly, the zero-shot performance of the LLaVA model is evaluated on the EMOTIC (adapted to a single-label task) and NCAER-S benchmarks, comparing the results with a baseline model consisting in a dual ResNet architecture complemented with a CCIM module. Secondly, SynthCAER is constructed, a synthetic dataset of 14 812 images generated using StableDiffusionXL from 529 contexts, whose categories correspond to the six basic emotions plus neutrality.
> 
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The results show mixed performance from the foundational model: LLaVA outperforms the baseline model on EMOTIC (F1 = 0.334 vs 0.164), but performs worse on NCAER-S (F1 = 0.148 vs 0.244), suggesting a greater reliance on contextual cues than on facial expression. On SynthCAER, both models improve in performance, indicating the presence of more prototypical visual schemas in the synthetic images. 
>
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It is concluded that foundation models show good potential, although they present issues such as a tendency to collapse into a few categories, such as joy and sadness. Preliminary indications of gender bias in the generative process are also detected, and a comprehensive analysis of this is proposed as a future line of research.
  

## 2- Establishing External Criteria for Updating a Meta-Analysis (2022)

### Estableciendo criterios externos para la actualización de un meta-análisis (2022)

  Master's Degree in Behaviour & Health Sciences Methodology Thesis | TFM Máster Interuniversitario en Metodología de las Ciencias del Comportamiento y de la Salud - Universidad Complutense de Madrid | Universidad Autónoma de Madrid | Universidad Nacional de Educación a Distancia
  
  Published as poster in the XVII Social & Health Sciences Methodology Congress (Teruel, Spain, 2022) | Presentado como póster en el XVII Congreso de Metodología de las Ciencias Sociales y de la Salud (Teruel, 2022)

  **Abstract**

> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The body of research that feeds evidence-based practice is so broad that meta-analysis, as a quantitative synthesis tool, has become a reference when making decisions. However, as time progresses, well-conducted meta-analyses become out of date, and one may wonder when it is worth publishing an update. It is important, similar to the sequential rules in primary studies, to establish an external criteria that help us decide when it is convenient to update a meta-analysis, especially when its results are not statistically significant.
>
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The objective of the present study is to develop a practical tool for the methodologist when deciding when to update a meta-analysis. That tool is based on two independent and complementary criteria: the power and the precision of the mean effect size estimate. A script has been developed for the statistical environment R that allows estimating how many studies would be necessary to reach: (a) a certain value of power, and/or (b) a specific reduction of the range of the confidence interval of the mean effect estimate. These calculations can be made for both fixed effect and random effects models.
  

## 3 - New technologies: Usage and mental health by teenagers. A meta-analysis about Effectiveness of Addiction Interventions (2022) 

### Nuevas tecnologías, uso y salud mental por parte de los adolescentes. Un meta-análisis sobre la efectividad de las intervenciones sobre la adicción (2022)

  Master's Degree in General Health Psychology Thesis | TFM Máster Universitario en Psicología General Sanitaria - Universidad Isabel I de Castilla
  
  Published in University's repository | Publicado en el Repositorio de la Universidad Isabel I de Castilla 

  **Abstract**

> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;New technologies are a daily element for most people, but there are cases of problematic use that can lead to addiction, with teenagers being the highest risk group. Cognitive behavioral therapy is often indicated as the most effective treatment for behavioral addictions, but there is little literature on interventions for technology addiction in teenagers.
>
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A systematic search was conducted in PsicoDoc, PsychInfo, PsychArticles, PubMed and Medline to identify recent interventions (from 2012 to 2022) on addiction to new technologies (internet, smartphone, social media and video games) in teenagers. A random effects meta-analysis has been performed to assess the effect of these interventions in comparison with a control group or another treatment.
> 
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A total of 15 primary studies have been included in the meta-analysis. A large overall effect of the treatments has been found on the clinical population (d = 0.920, 95% CI between 0.603 and 1.238), while no reduction in addictive symptomatology has been found in preventive studies on the general population (d = 0.048, 95% CI between −0.505 and 0.600). No effect of the potential coded moderating variables was found (e.g. intervention or new technology type).
> 
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There is still no consensus on the concept of new technologies addiction, which complicates its detection and treatment. The results of the meta-analysis should be taken with caution given the small sample size and quality of the included studies. More research is still needed to establish which interventions are more effective on teenagers.
  

## 4 - Interpersonal Conflict in Children's Animated Films. A Comparative Study of Classic and Postmodern Cinema (2020)

### El conflicto interpersonal en el cine de animación infantil. Un estudio comparativo entre el cine clásico y el cine postmoderno (2020)

  Master's Degree in Social Psychology Thesis | TFM Máster en Psicología Social - Universidad Complutense de Madrid
  
  Published in the Journal of Social Psychology Master, 8 | Publicado en la Revista del Máster de Psicología Social, 8. https://www.ucm.es/master-psicologia-social/numero-8-19-20

  **Abstract**

> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Animated films have a strong influence on children’s socialization processes. The shift in thinking brought about by the postmodern perspective has altered the way stories are told, which in turn affects socialization processes. Analyzing the psychosocial factors present in animated films is of great importance, as children acquire behaviors through this medium. The objective of this analysis is to describe how interpersonal conflicts are portrayed and to compare this portrayal in classic cinema (up to 1968) and postmodern cinema (from 1990 onward). Interpersonal conflict is a complex phenomenon of great importance in cinema, as it is the core and driving force of subplots and character relationships. The reference points for the analysis of conflicts were the hierarchical structure and the power imbalance between characters, as well as the use of violence to resolve conflicts and the representation of gender roles. These elements shape how characters of both genders manage conflicts. A cinematic content analysis was conducted on a sample of films, focusing on the behavior of the protagonists and their developed subplots.
> 
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The results reveal a significant shift in the portrayal of interpersonal conflicts from one era to another. In classic cinema, the source of conflicts stems from the differing roles characteristic of Parsonian functionalism, whereas in contemporary cinema, conflicts arise when the assigned role is called into question. Hierarchical structure is a highly significant element in animated cinema, and it shapes the conflict strategies of the characters involved. While in classic cinema the hierarchy remains unchanged, in contemporary cinema it undergoes a shift due to the protagonist’s actions. Furthermore, the portrayal of women’s roles has changed drastically from one era of cinema to another. In classical cinema, women could be the catalyst for conflict but played a completely passive role in its resolution, whereas in contemporary cinema they have gradually gained autonomy, to the point of managing conflicts in a manner similar to male
protagonists. The use of violence in conflict resolution has hardly changed from one period to another. In both eras, violence is key to the development of relationships between characters and the resolution of conflict. However, in postmodern cinema, there are instances that attempt to condemn violence, though they continue to resort to it, as its comic element makes it highly appealing to the viewer. Finally, in postmodern cinema, the journey appears, used as a narrative device to develop conflicts. The journey facilitates encounters, as well as obstacles that must be overcome cooperatively and that ultimately transform the relationships between characters.
> 
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The shift in how conflicts are portrayed in animated films is a direct result of a change in the mindset of their creators. In the classical era, film served an indoctrinating function aimed at fostering conformity, whereas contemporary film seems to empower young people to defend their individuality. Similarly, the shift in the representation of gender roles will presumably have a positive impact on young people’s expectations regarding conflict, as they will view both genders as equally capable. Violence is a controversial element in animation, as there is no consensus on its potential influence on children’s behavior and, therefore, on its use as a conflict resolution strategy.


## 5 - Differences in Psychological Characteristics in Handball Referees (2019)

### Diferencias en las características psicológicas en árbitros de balonmano (2019)

  Bachelor's Degree in Psychology Thesis | TFG Grado en Psicología - Universidad de Salamanca

  **Abstract**

> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sports psychology is an emerging field within psychology, but one that has neglected the role of the referee. The referee is essential in any competitive sport, and—just as with any athlete—there are a number of psychological factors that influence the referee’s performance and well-being. We administered the “Psychological Characteristics Related to Sports Performance” questionnaire (CPRD; Gimeno, Buceta, and Pérez-Llantada, 2001) to 102 referees from different regions of Spain. The questionnaire measures five constructs: stress management, motivation, mental skills, team cohesion, and influence on performance evaluation.
>
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We found significantly higher scores among more experienced referees in stress management and mental ability; however, when comparing by category, we found differences only in mental ability. Similarly, older referees had significantly higher scores than younger ones in stress management and influence on performance evaluation.
> 
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We can conclude that more experienced referees possess greater skills when it comes to handling refereeing duties. However, we observe that the CPRD is not a particularly suitable instrument for evaluating referees, as the scales for motivation and team cohesion do not align with the reality of refereeing. It is important to continue researching referee psychology in order to develop effective training programs that help improve referees’ performance.
